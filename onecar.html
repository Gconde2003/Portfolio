Polly & Mothy

One Car Challenge - Final Render

This piece is a tiny, stylized world set in the rain. It’s a whimsical scene where two young moths meet for the first time, driving their little leaf car through the drops. I wanted it to feel soft, playful, and a bit romantic, like a small story unfolding in the middle of a rainy night.

Leaf Car – Turnaround

The car is built from leaves, twigs, small branches, amber, insect parts, and acorn caps. Materials the moths could find and use in their tiny world. While it has organic shapes, it feels solid and practical, designed to move through the rain-soaked landscape.

Polly & Mothy– Turnaround

I decided to add the characters to the scene to bring more life to the render. The car alone didn’t fully tell the story, so the moths help give it context and emotion.

When designing these two characters inspired by the moth, I imagined them as opposites: one wild, free-spirited and restless, the other gentle, and almost domestic. Together they balance each other, like two sides of the same story.

So, Polly is representing Bombyx mori. She is gentle and soft, with light, delicate colors. She feels calm and composed, almost like a quiet companion in this tiny, whimsical world.

And Mothy, representing Bombyx mandarina, is wilder and more untamed, with deeper, earthy tones of brown and amber. Her energy contrasts with Polly, bringing a sense of adventure and spontaneity to the scene.
